Context & Stakes

LEGO was in the middle of a decade-long transformation from a toy manufacturer into a digital enterprise with tightly integrated global processes and platforms. On the back end, the enterprise platform was world-class. On the front end, the global online shop still felt like an afterthought — a patchwork of regional sites that didn't reflect the LEGO brand or how families actually shopped.

The Challenge

UX & Platform Blockers

  • Four-row nav with legacy taxonomy — "Themes / Categories / Age Range / Exclusives / What's New / Sales" — didn't match how people shopped
  • Cart and checkout had different rules per region — fragile, hard to change
  • Mobile felt like a scaled-down desktop, not a first-class experience

Governance & Scale Blockers

  • Design decisions lived in scattered decks and emails — no single source of truth
  • Dozens of global stakeholders, shifting technical roadmap, no shared framework
  • Promotions, loyalty, gift cards had no documented rules — lived in tribal knowledge

The Core Tension: LEGO's enterprise platform was the transformation's backbone. The online shop had to respect those constraints completely — while finally feeling as playful and intuitive as LEGO itself.

Engagement Kickoff — June 2015

BCG engaged to rethink LEGO.com as part of the wider enterprise transformation. Scope defined: re-architect the global online shop across desktop, tablet, and mobile, aligned to LEGO's SAP platform and multi-country operations.

Global IA & Navigation Architecture

Four header rows collapsed into two. New taxonomy — Sets / Bricks / Accessories / Explore — defined based on how customers actually browse. Search, account, region-switcher, wishlist, and bag preview standardized across all devices and markets.

Cart, Checkout & PRD Development

Full PRD and flow ownership for shopping bag, sign-in, addresses, shipping, payments, and confirmation across desktop and mobile. 140+ page annotated working-team deck. Every promo, error, and regional variation documented explicitly — no tribal knowledge.

Promotions, Loyalty & Gift Cards

Documented how discounts, loyalty IDs, and gift cards appear and calculate through cart and checkout, with connections to LEGO's order-to-cash and finance rules. Reduced one-off promos and made campaigns repeatable for marketing.

Design Authority & Stakeholder Governance

Built Design Authority materials with BCG to walk senior LEGO stakeholders through objectives, principles, and UX decisions. Turned opinion-driven debates into structured decision points and got alignment across LEGO, BCG, and external agencies.

Launch & Lasting Impact — June 2016

New global shop live. The patterns defined for navigation, checkout, and governance became LEGO's digital playbook. The wider transformation — including the online shop work — was later cited by MIT, Harvard, and HBR as a gold-standard model for digital strategy.
