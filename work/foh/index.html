Context & Stakes

ABG bought Frederick's of Hollywood out of bankruptcy with a clear thesis: bring it back as a digital-only brand with a sharper point of view. They brought in Megan Fox as co-owner and ambassador to reintroduce Frederick's to an audience that cared more about Instagram than malls. The problem was the tech stack: heavy, expensive, and built for a catalogue era, not for a social-driven celebrity relaunch with a hard PR deadline.

The Challenge

Platform Blockers

  • Legacy platform required large dev teams for any change
  • Shopify Plus (2016) had a monolithic architecture, less flexible for enterprise
  • Back-office sync required custom workarounds
  • Vendor lock-in risk for a PE portfolio brand

Business Blockers

  • Hard PR deadline: Megan Fox campaign couldn't wait for a slow migration
  • Marketing needed self-service; couldn't rely on IT for every campaign
  • Traffic spikes from press and celebrity coverage had to be handled without drama
  • ABG needed proof this could scale to other portfolio brands

The Core Tension: Shopify Plus was the right direction: lean, fast, campaign-friendly, but it was young, and this was a very public proof of concept. Getting it wrong would have set back Shopify's enterprise story and Frederick's relaunch simultaneously.
