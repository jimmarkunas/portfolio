The Vision: Internet TV From Scratch

ZEVO was an attempt to answer one question: what would television look like if you designed it for the internet from the beginning, instead of trying to cram cable TV into a browser? The concept was a unified network that felt the same across every device â€” TV, laptop, phone â€” where linear and on-demand lived side-by-side, and where the content itself was interactive.

One Network Across All Screens

The core product idea was a unified internet TV network that behaved the same way across devices. When you turned ZEVO on, you dropped into a live stream; from there you could flip to on-demand, see what friends were watching, or interact with products without leaving the experience.

  • 24/7 linear stream for lean-back watching
  • On-demand catalog for catch-up and deep dives
  • Shared identity and state across TV, laptop, and phone

Second-Screen Control & Sync

One of ZEVO's distinctive product bets was that the phone would become the remote and interaction layer for TV. We treated the TV as the main canvas and the phone as the place where you could control playback, see metadata, and interact without cluttering the big screen.

  • Phone app could discover and sync with current stream
  • Control playback and channel selection from your hand
  • Surface show details, cast, and interactive elements
  • Built to feel natural for multitasking between TV and mobile

Interactive Commerce

Another core product thesis was that advertising would move from interruptive spots to interactive moments inside the content itself. ZEVO's interactive commerce layer let viewers tap on highlighted objects in the frame and see product details, comments, and purchase options in real time.

  • Items in a scene were tagged and outlined subtly on screen
  • Tapping opened a panel with details, social comments, and buy action

Platform Architecture

Behind the UX, ZEVO was an IPTV platform that delivered live-streaming and on-demand video over the open internet instead of through traditional cable or satellite. We chose an HTML5 and JavaScript front end for flexibility, layered on top of a LAMP-based backend and streaming infrastructure that looked more like a web platform than a traditional broadcast stack.

Technical Stack

  • Internet-delivered streaming: Live and on-demand over HTTP, not cable/satellite infrastructure
  • Single service layer: Identity, content metadata, and interactive events in one API
  • Ad and commerce services: Designed to plug into existing networks for billing and fulfillment
  • HTML5 front end: Reusable across web, mobile web, and embedded TV browsers

Outcome & Founder Edge

ZEVO never launched as a live network. I shut it down when it was clear we couldn't raise a round big enough to build the product properly.
